CamSmart

Abstract

Police and security departments all over the world have realized that the best way to reduce crime is to have good deterrence mechanisms. Areas that are well lit and have surveillance cameras deters anyone with a malicious intent. Doing so is exponentially cheaper and effective than having a guard at every corner. And so departments/individuals/organizations have set up thousands of surveillance cameras. Even then, there is a small but considerable amount of crime that still happens and to manually monitor all those video streams is an impossible task. And so researchers have developed techniques and algorithms to automate this process, and to respond immediately to any emergency situation.

The use case of a surveillance system is vast and covers a range of behaviours and rules, we will be focusing on developing a model that looks to identify knifepoint/gunpoint situations. The model will be trained to identify different types of weapons and the program will also be trained to look for fire threats around the vicinity. This system will also help identify individuals in the premises by verifiying with our database. The model haas the functionality of identifiying if an individual has worn a mask.
